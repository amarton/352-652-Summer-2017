Interviewing Data

Eight Steps

1. Think of a question

2. Find data to interview

3. Skeptically investigate the data's accuracy

4. Translate your questions into langugage the data understands

Eight Steps

5. Put the answers in context

6. Repeat 1-5 as needed

7. Translate your findings into language people understand

8. Before publication, have someone else reproduce your work

1. Think of interview questions

How did Germany win the World Cup?

Get specific

Were they better on offense than other teams?

Get more specific

Did they have a higher percentage of shots on target than other teams? Were they better at passing than other teams?

2. Find data to interview

Easy: Download data. Examples: Census.gov American FactFinder, Data.gov, Baltimore City Open Data

Harder: Scrape. Tools: Table Capture, Import.io, Tabula

Even harder: FOIA/PIA. Example: Langley Park pedestrian deaths

DIY. Examples: Other Redskins, Fatal Force, Drone Strikes

3. Skeptically investigate the data's accuracy

How do you know that?

Who collected it? Do they have an agenda?

How was it collected? Does the methodology seem reliable?

Is this the original source?

3. Skeptically investigate the data's accuracy

Are there alternative sources of the same information? Does this dataset match those?

What does the data actually tell you? Don't assume, read the documentation.

Are there missing values? Are there values that seem improbable? Use formulas to look for problems.

4. Translate your questions into langugage the data understands

Did Germany have a higher percentage of shots on target on other teams?

"Attempts on Target (Column E)"/"Shots(Column D)"

In first row of Column H, write formula

=E2/D2

Copy it into subsequent rows

Sort Column H

5. Put the answers in context

Calculate minimum, maximum, median, average and rank.

What is newsworthy? More, less, change, relationships, surprising.

5. Put the answers in context

72.45 percent of Germany's shot attempts were on target.

Is that good? Bad?

Median: 59.73, Max: 72.45, Min: 42.5, Germany Rank: 1.

5. Put the answers in context

Germany: 98 shots during tournament. Iran: 22 shots during tournament.

Is it misleading to say Germany had almost 5 times as many shots as Iran?

Yes. Germany played 7 games, Iran 3.

Meaningful: Germany averaged 14 shots per game, Iran 7.3

6. Repeat 1-5 as needed

This process is not always linear.

Answers bring up new questions.

Answers reveal unseen flaws in the data set.

7. Repeat 1-5 as needed

This process is not always linear.

Answers bring up new questions.

Answers reveal unseen flaws in the data set.

7. Translate and share findings

Write your finding in plain English. Include necessary context.

“Germany put itself in a position to score more often than any other team in the World Cup. Nearly three out of four -- 72.45 percent -- of its shots were on goal, a higher rate than any other team in the tournament.”

Only after you've reached this point do you determine how to visualize it.

8. Before publication, have someone else check your work.

Give a colleague your data, findings and steps you took to produce those findings.

Ask them to repeat your work, looking for errors in logic.
